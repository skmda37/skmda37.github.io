A Little About Me

Hi, I'm Stefan, welcome to my personal website! Currently, I'm pursuing a Computer Science Ph.D. at Ludwig-Maximilians-University Munich, where I specialize in interpretable machine learning. My objective is to create AI models that are transparent and interpretable. I spent the first two years of my Ph.D. focusing on post-hoc explanations of image classifiers using tools from applied harmonic analysis. Black box explanations can be useful for model debugging and bias identification. However, more recently, I've shifted towards research on inherently interpretable models because black box explanations have various issues that I believe cannot be overcome. Broadly speaking, my research focuses on trustworthy machine learning, particularly for computer vision.

Something My Resume Doesn't Tell You

Everyone has a passion and a subject close to their heart. For me, it's mental health. I am committed to discussing, sharing, and learning about various mental health experiences. Sadly, a large number of people are not mentally healthy, and even more concerning, many are unaware of this fact. After spending considerable time reflecting and addressing mental health, I have become convinced of two valuable notions: (1) Mental health is contagious; addressing one's own mental health not only benefits the individual but also promotes well-being among others. (2) The mental health status of people can undergo a phase transition, implying that if k sufficiently large out of every n people were mentally healthy, a domino effect would lead to a mentally healthy population, and a much better world. I openly share this because I like working and connecting with people who are curious and open-minded about mental health.
